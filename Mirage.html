Mirage


Sally C. is a student.

"She is a top tiered student, has a few friends growing up with her, doesn't talk much, but is a nice person overall.”

Sally has been feeling weird lately.

Her grades in high school have always been decent, but every time she receives the test score, some kind of conditioned tension rushes to her head. Her heart beat increases, her breathing becomes rapid, and she eagerly craves but yet does not know what to expect. She attributes these to her habits: she is a good student throughout her life and is concerned about keeping up with that image. Sally doesn’t think it is a bad thing to be disciplined, but now this tension seems to have spread to other fields.

A few days ago, Sally was on the street, alone on the whole asphalt road by herself. The noonday sunshine sunk people in deep swoon; exuberant greens on both sides of the road extended their branches, which could almost hang down to her face. Sally took a step and walked into the middle of the road. She glanced to her left in the direction of the coming traffic, which was so neatly lined up that it seemed to come to life.

She could see through a sheet of coloured glass, or the thin shield, seeing clearly the drivers’ faces and gestures.

For no reason, Sally felt pulled back to the morning classroom, with the unceasing ticking sounds from the clock above. Her teacher’s face became ambiguous behind the rostrum, same as the drivers’ hidden indifference vision. Suddenly, all students in the classroom raised their heads at the same time; Are they all looking at her? She questioned. For a few short seconds, she was dizzy and could not even feel her breath. She turned her head back and walked down the tree-covered street. It wasn't until she crawled back into her room and smelt the aroma of stir-fry coming from outside that she seemed to finally relieve.

“I dreaded the eyes of the cars”, Sally murmured to herself, as if their sights were shooting at my body.

Why Cars and Street?

Sometimes mechanical objects give people a sense of "unfamiliar" horror for no reason, just as humans are reflexively afraid of snakes, regardless the fear was in their genes or not.

For the social anxiety groups, eye contact with the strangers, exposing under the observation of others, and the fear subconsciously influenced by negative thoughts, would all prevent them from maintaining basic social interactions. These anxieties and fears more forcefully projected on the objects represent the human.

There was nothing wrong with the cold inorganic metal. It’s just the eyes from the other side of the windows might have been the sword that pierced the little patch of skin behind Achilles' ankle.

They walk on zebra crossings full of parked cars that have become human beings, and in the SAD's mind, the two things merge into an absurd Frankenstein. Like how a chinese folktale goes, “victims of snake bites, might even be scared of ropes for a decade.”

SADs live in a game of survival, where the eyes of wolves twinkle behind the bushes, staring at the poor passerby. When the wolves attack humans, our "fight-or-flight response" kicks in. For SAD, seemingly “abnormal” reactions like sweaty palms, rapid heartbeat, and even a tremor, is only a natural instinct of the body.

On the End: Where we are not living in a binary world, there’s no normal or not normal

What is SAD?

"Social anxiety", also known as social phobia, has become a commonly used slang. "Social anxiety" or embarrassing scenes are everywhere. Public speaking, called on by teachers, listening to your boss singing in karaoke, or entangling with the sight of strangers may be all defined as mild triggers.

It’s just for SAD that their normal lives have been severely affected. For most people, speaking on a conference may give them a sweaty palm at most, but for SAD patients, this may be a catalyst for their suicide.

With reference to the psychological theory that “Neurological tendencies aren't binary ticker boxes, they're just scaled differently on a spectrum." , it is a human right to feel embarrassed. In any case, everyone has a manifestation of individual differences, just some people have a bit more.

Respect differences. Accept differences.
